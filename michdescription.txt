Michelangelo di Lodovico Buonarroti Simoni, typically known as just Michelangelo, is an Italian scultor and painter that was born in Tuscany Italy. He was born on March 6, 1475, and died on February 18, 1564.
<br><br>
He was one of the biggest influences on the development of Western art, and one of the greatest artistic people to ever exist. He is a contender for the title of the archetypal Renaissance man.
<br><br>
A number of his works rank amongst some of the most famous artistic pieces ever to exist. He created the Pieta and David before he even hit 30, and created the scenes of Genesis on the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel. He also painted The Last Judgment on the altar wall, as well as designing the Laurentian Library.
